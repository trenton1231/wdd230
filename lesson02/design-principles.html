Repetition

Target

Target uses the principle of repetition very well. Throughout the site, you can see the iconic red ball used in various fashions and instantly know that you must be on Target's website. With the use of red repeated throughout the website, your are further constantly reminded that you are on Target's website.

Visual Hierarchy

Apple

The first thing you see upon loading Apple's website is their newest product for this year. It is the most important thing and what they assume most people will be looking for. The name of said product is next, and finally their catchy phrase for whatever product it is this time around. Everything about that product is accessible by clicking on the biggest part of the website, the products photo. They've done a fantastic job at setting up visual hierarchy in my opinion.

Hick's Law

BestBuy

Overall, BestBuy has done a pretty good job of following Hick's Law. While there are a lot of choices, they are given to you slowly in an effort to reduce the overwhelming options when it comes to various electronics and the choices that a consumer has with them. I searched for televisions and while I was given the option to pick everything I was filtering for, I was also shown a nice selection of sizes to start at. From there, I was able to filter much less options based on the size of potential television I wanted.
